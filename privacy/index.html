Privacy Policy

Effective date: 16 July 2026

App: Sirran (iPhone, Apple Watch, Android phone, Wear OS)

Publisher: Baris Demircan

Contact: barisdemircan@outlook.com.tr

The worship promise: everything you do in worship — prayer logs, Quran reading and notes, du'a book, tasbih counts, qada ledger, zakat figures, voice recordings — stays on your device. Sirran has no user accounts, no analytics, and no server of its own that receives your data. The free version shows ads served by Google AdMob, and subscriptions are processed by Google Play; those two Google services process some data as described below. Sirran Plus removes ads entirely — for subscribers the advertising SDK is never even started.

What Sirran never collects

  • We do not collect, store, or transmit your worship data off your device — not your prayer marks, Quran positions or margin notes, du'as, tasbih counts, qada ledger, hifz records, zakat figures, or recordings.
  • We do not use analytics or tracking SDKs, and we have no user accounts or sign-in.
  • We do not operate a backend: apart from the services named in this policy (AdMob ads in the free version, Google Play for purchases, OpenStreetMap for the optional Nearby Mosques lookup), the app makes no network requests.
  • We do not sell your data. We never see it in the first place.

Advertising in the free version (Google AdMob)

The free version of Sirran is supported by ads served through Google AdMob. To serve and measure ads, Google's advertising SDK processes data on Google's side, which can include your device's advertising identifier, IP address, coarse location derived from it, and ad-interaction data. Where you have consented (or outside regions that require consent), ads may be personalized based on this data. Details are in Google's Privacy Policy and how Google uses data from partner apps.

  • In the EEA, UK, and Switzerland the app first shows Google's consent form (UMP). If you decline personalization, any ads shown are limited accordingly.
  • Ads never interrupt worship. No ad is shown over the Quran reader, during tasbih or adhkar, or around alarms and prayer alerts, and ads are frequency-limited.
  • Your worship data plays no part in ads. Nothing you do in the app — what you read, log, or count — is shared with Google or used for ad targeting.
  • Sirran Plus subscribers see no ads, and the advertising SDK is not initialized for them at all. You can also limit personalization system-wide in your device's Google settings ("Delete advertising ID" / "Opt out of Ads Personalization").

Sirran Plus subscriptions (Google Play)

Sirran Plus is an optional subscription sold through Google Play Billing. Payment is handled entirely by Google Play: we never see your card details, name, or address. The app receives only the subscription's status (a purchase token) to unlock Plus features, and your purchase history is processed by Google under Google's Privacy Policy. You can manage or cancel the subscription any time in the Play Store; the worship core of Sirran is free and remains fully functional without it.

Nearby Mosques (optional)

When you open Nearby Mosques, Sirran sends your approximate location to OpenStreetMap's public Overpass service to find mosques near you, and shows the results in the app. This happens only when you use that feature. We do not store your location or the results, there is no account, and no tracking is involved. Tapping a result opens your maps app for directions.

On-device data and permissions

The app uses the following only on your device, to provide its features:

  • Location (optional): Used on your device to calculate accurate prayer times and the Qibla direction. If you do not grant location permission, the app falls back to a manually selected city. Your location is not stored by us and is never sent off the device by Sirran itself. (If ads are shown, Google may derive coarse location from your IP address as described above.)
  • Notifications (optional): Used to deliver local prayer-time and feature reminders scheduled on your device, in your device's language. There is no remote push; no notification content is sent to a server.
  • Microphone and speech recognition (optional, off by default): Used for the opt-in voice features. Voice Tasbih and the Hifz Voice Coach use on-device recognition only — they are disabled when on-device recognition is unavailable and never fall back to network recognition, and they do not store audio. The In Your Voice library records du'a recordings you choose to make; these files are stored only on your device and are never uploaded.
  • Camera (optional, Android): Used solely by the Qibla Horizon and crescent-watch views as a live viewfinder behind the on-screen markers. The preview only passes through the screen — nothing is captured, recorded, stored, or transmitted, and the camera is active only while that screen is open.
  • Do Not Disturb access (optional, off by default, Android): Used solely for the opt-in Quiet Hours feature, which silences your phone around each prayer and restores it afterwards. This is a device setting change only; no information is read, stored, or transmitted.
  • Calendar (optional, off by default): if you enable "prayer times in your calendar", the app writes prayer events into a separate, device-local calendar that never syncs to any account. Turning the feature off removes the events. The app does not read your other calendars' contents in this version.
  • Zakat Vault (optional): the wealth figures you enter for the zakat calculator are stored only on your device, in an encrypted file protected by your phone's hardware keystore. They are never transmitted, and we could not read them even if we wanted to — there is no server.
  • Sirran Keyboard (optional, Android): if you enable the bundled phrase keyboard in system settings, it only inserts the phrase you tap into the text field. It does not read, log, store, or transmit anything you type.
  • Apple Watch / Wear OS sync: Prayer preferences, tasbih counts, the qada total, and your wird routine's name are shared between the phone and the watch on your devices / your Apple or Google account's device sync. This is device-to-device data, not collected by us.

Quran content

The Quran text in the app is "Tanzil Quran Text" (Simple and Uthmani) from the Tanzil Project (tanzil.net), used under the Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 license with in-app attribution. The bundled translations are "The Meaning of the Glorious Koran" by Marmaduke Pickthall (1930, English) and the original "Hak Dini Kur'an Dili" meal by Elmalili Muhammed Hamdi Yazir (1935–1939, Turkish), both in the public domain and shown with in-app attribution. All texts are bundled with the app and read on your device; using the Quran reader does not transmit any data, and no ad is ever shown over it.

Children's privacy

Sirran is a religious utility app and is not directed at children. It does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children. The free version contains advertising served by Google AdMob, and an optional subscription is offered through Google Play; there are no social or user-generated-content features.

Data security and retention

Sirran operates no server, so there is no Sirran-side storage of your information. App settings and worship data are stored locally on your device and are removed when you uninstall the app. Data processed by Google (ad serving in the free version, purchase processing for subscriptions) is held by Google under its own policies and retention rules; Sirran cannot access it beyond the subscription status needed to unlock Plus.

Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices change, this policy and the app store data disclosures will be updated before that change ships. This version (16 July 2026) added the advertising and subscription sections when the free tier became ad-supported and Sirran Plus went on sale.
